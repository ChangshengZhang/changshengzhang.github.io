Research Experience

Bayesian Statistics

  • School of Economics and Management, Tsinghua University (Apr. 2015 -Present)

  • Advisor: Associate Professor Chun Liu.

  • Developed a novel Bayesian algorithm based on the Markov Chain Monte Carlo method for inference in dynamic regression models in Python.

  • Outperformed in predicting GDP inflation compared to other static Bayesian variable selection and regularisation method.

  • Currently working on how to seek portfolio managers’ time-varying alpha based on their long-term historic returns according to Capital Asset Pricing Model.

Netwrok Traffic Classification

  • Research Institute of Information Technology, Tsinghua University (Sep. 2014 -Present)

  • Advisor: Professor Jun Li.

  • Designed a classifier which could detect Skype’s network traffics in C and Python based on several machine learning algorithms including SVM, KNN, C4.5 and etc.

  • Applied the classifier to classify more than 10 GB of offline traffic data and real-time traffic data with 92% and 83% accuracy rate accordingly.

  • Currently working on how to optimize some general machine learning models to classify encrypted traffic including but not limited to Skype, Tecent WeChat.

RoboCup@Home

  • Tsinghua University, Beijing & João Pessoa, Brazil (Apr. 2013 - Jul. 2014)

  • Designed a family service and assistive robot which could do housework autonomously in a complex environment.

  • Developed a speech recognition and synthesis system on Linux based on the API provided by CMU Sphinx, enabling the robot to interact with humans in English and Chinese with 95% and 80% accuracy rates correspondingly.

  • Organized weekly technical meeting and coordinated team’s work and project schedules as a team leader.

  • Won the 10th out of 19 teams(10/19) in the RoboCup@Home League, the largest international annual competition for autonomous service robots, in July 2014 in João Pessoa, Brazil.

Distributed Transportation Simulation

  • Institute of System Engineering, Tsinghua University (Sep. 2013 - Jun. 2014)

  • Developed a distributed simulation system to handle large-scale traffic flow simulations, consisting of more than 30,000 lines of high-quality C++ and C# code.

  • Visualized the progress of simulation as a video game by using the API provided by Microsoft Direct X.

  • Won 1st prize out of 73 teams(2/73) in the 32nd ”Challenge Cup”Competition at Tsinghua University in April 2014.
